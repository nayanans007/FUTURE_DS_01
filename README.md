# Superstore Sales Performance Analysis

## Context

Businesses generate large amounts of sales data every day, but raw data alone cannot support decision-making unless it is properly analyzed and visualized.

This project focuses on analyzing Superstore sales data to identify:

* Important business trends
* Profitable categories
* High-performing products
* Overall sales growth

Using **Microsoft Excel** and **Power BI**, the raw sales dataset was cleaned, transformed, and converted into an interactive dashboard that provides meaningful business insights and supports data-driven decision-making.

# Key Insights

## 1. Strong Business Growth (2014–2017)

* Sales increased from approximately **$484K in 2014** to **$733K in 2017**
* Overall growth exceeded **50%** over four years

---

## 2. Recovery After 2015 Decline

* Sales declined by approximately **2.9% in 2015**
* Strong recovery followed:

  * **27.7% growth in 2016**
  * **22.2% growth in 2017**

---

## 3. Technology Was the Best-Performing Category

### Performance:

* **$836K** in total sales
* **$145K** in total profit

Despite having fewer orders than Office Supplies, Technology generated significantly higher revenue and profitability per order.

---

## 4. Furniture Category Had Low Profitability

### Performance:

* **$742K** in sales
* Only **~$18K** in profit

### Possible Reasons:

* High operational costs
* Heavy discounts
* Low profit margins
* Inefficient pricing strategies

This was one of the most critical findings from the analysis.

---

## 5. Office Supplies Had the Highest Order Volume

* Approximately **6,000 orders**
* Lower average order value
* Generated stable revenue and strong overall profitability

---

## 6. Premium Products Contributed Strongly to Revenue

Products such as:

> **Canon ImageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier**

generated very high revenue and profit despite having only a few orders.

This suggests that premium products contribute significantly to overall business performance.

---

## 7. Some Products Generated Sales but Little Profit

Example:

> **HON 5400 Series Task Chairs for Big and Tall**

* Generated approximately **$21.87K** in sales
* Produced almost zero profit

This indicates that certain products may reduce overall profitability due to poor pricing or high associated costs.

# Interpretation

The dashboard analysis shows that the business experienced strong long-term growth and increasing customer demand between 2014 and 2017.

The company generated approximately:

* **$2.30 million** in total sales
* Nearly **$286K** in total profit

The analysis highlights that the **Technology** category was the strongest business segment, generating the highest revenue and profit despite fewer orders than Office Supplies.

In contrast, the **Furniture** category revealed a major profitability issue. Although sales revenue was high, profits remained extremely low, showing that strong sales alone do not guarantee business success if pricing and operational costs are poorly managed.

The dashboard also demonstrated the importance of product-level analysis. Some products generated very high profits with only a few orders, while others generated revenue without meaningful profitability.

These findings can support smarter decisions related to:

* Product strategy
* Pricing optimization
* Inventory planning
* Future investments
